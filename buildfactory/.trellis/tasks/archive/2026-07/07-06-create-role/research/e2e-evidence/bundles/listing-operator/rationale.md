# Why hire a listing operator

**The class of work**: getting finished products LIVE on third-party
marketplaces — Gumroad, Amazon KDP, and similar storefronts. Our standing
objective requires "a publicly reachable listing URL" as its success
condition, and this final mile has no owner today.

**Why it keeps failing / has nowhere to go**: Gumroad and KDP expose no
publishing API — listing means driving a real logged-in browser session
(computer use) through multi-step seller flows: account dashboards, upload
forms, pricing pages, tax interviews. I have had no department to send
"publish this on Gumroad" to. Builder's charter ends at producing the
artifact; growth's work is demand-side (SEO, content, distribution) — neither
owns operating a seller account, and a KILLED listing attempt costs more than
tokens: repeated sloppy retries against a marketplace login risk flagging the
account itself, which is shared company infrastructure.

**Which existing role came closest, and the axis of the shortfall**: builder.
But the shortfall is judgment posture, not tools — builder already has the
full MCP set including the browser. What builder lacks, and what a charter
must set, is the operating stance for account-bearing work: treating account
standing as the scarce resource, knowing when to abort a flow rather than
retry into a risk flag, and producing evidence (the public URL) rather than
"I filled the form". Bolting a listing skill onto builder would put
maker-judgment and account-operator-judgment in one head: the maker's bias is
to push until it works, which is exactly wrong on someone else's platform.

**Why now**: the current objective (ship one paid info-product, success = live
listing URL) makes this the blocking last step of real in-flight work, not a
speculative future need.
