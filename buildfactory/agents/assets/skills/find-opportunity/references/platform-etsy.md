# Finding an info-product opportunity on Etsy

Read this once you've picked Etsy. It makes steps 4–5 of the main method concrete for
Etsy. Etsy fits **text/layout-driven digital downloads** a design-light author can
produce: printable planners, habit/budget trackers, worksheets, checklists,
spreadsheets, resume/CV templates, fill-in Canva text templates.

## Two facts that shape everything here

1. **Etsy is a search-discovery market, not an audience market.** Buyers find listings
   by keyword match (title/tags) plus a listing-quality score — you don't need a
   following. So an opportunity = a keyword with real search, beatable supply, and room
   for a brand-new listing to rank.
2. **By 2026 Etsy is flooded with generic AI-made downloads — generic is dead.** A plain
   "printable budget planner" won't move even when demand is real. Opportunities have to
   be **hyper-specific**, anchored to a concrete buyer identity, occasion, or software.

## Step 4 here — prove people already pay (Etsy reads)

Read search-vs-supply, then read real sales.
```text
Research question: "For the Etsy keyword '<keyword>' (a digital-download niche): report (1) approximate MONTHLY ETSY
  SEARCHES (eRank/EverBee 'Average Searches'), (2) the NUMBER OF COMPETING LISTINGS (Etsy search-page 'X
  results' + eRank competition), (3) keyword difficulty if available, (4) head vs long-tail. Then for the TOP
  ~10-15 listings report price, the 'X sold' count, number of reviews, stars, Bestseller/Star-Seller badge, and
  EverBee estimated monthly sales if available. Report numbers, not adjectives."
Expected evidence: "Keyword: monthly searches | competing listings | difficulty | head-vs-longtail; PLUS a top-listings
  table with price / 'X sold' / reviews / stars / badges / est. monthly sales."
```
- **Keep (demand real, findable):** searches roughly **≥500/month**, supply **not saturated** (about **< 100,000** competing listings, eRank "green" competition), low difficulty — **and** several top listings selling in the hundreds/thousands with real review depth (money is moving).
- **Drop (too thin):** trivially low search (e.g. ~22/month), or top listings with only single-digit/tens "sold" — interest, not demand.
- **Drop (saturated):** a broad head term, or a niche already carrying hundreds of established stores.

## Step 5 here — a beatable gap, and no generic trap (Etsy reads)

The same top-listing read gives you the wedge, and a second Goal keeps you honest about
the AI-flood.
```text
Research question: "For '<candidate product>' on Etsy: is the top of search dominated by GENERIC, interchangeable,
  likely-AI-generated listings, OR by hyper-specific listings anchored to a concrete buyer/occasion/software?
  Report the generic-saturation degree. Mine the reviews and Q&A of the top listings for what buyers WISH
  existed (an audience/occasion/format not yet served). Propose the most specific defensible angle."
Expected evidence: "A generic-vs-specific read of the top listings, quoted 'wish it had' gaps, and one hyper-specific
  product angle."
```
- **Keep (proven but beatable):** top listings sell **and** at least one top-position listing has low reviews, a mediocre rating (≤ ~4.0★), or recurring complaints — a gap. A listing with strong estimated revenue but **fewer than ~50 reviews** is a newer best-seller not yet walled off by a review moat: a strong keep.
- **Keep the angle only if hyper-specific:** "nurse night-shift budget planner", not "budget planner".
- **Drop (saturated):** top all 4.5★+ with thousands of reviews/sales at rock-bottom prices, Bestseller badges everywhere — no room.
- **Drop (generic trap):** a bare generic category with an AI-flooded top of search — even with real demand.
- **Drop (price suicide):** trying to win with **$1** listings — they don't signal quality and drag your listing score.

## Can a new listing even rank? (Etsy's discovery gate)

Because Etsy is pure search discovery, only pursue keywords where a **brand-new,
zero-review listing** can realistically rank.
```text
Research question: "For candidate Etsy keyword '<keyword>': is it a broad head term or a 3+ word long-tail? What exact
  long-tail phrasings do current top listings use in their titles (and visible tags)? Are the top listings
  entrenched high-review/Bestseller shops, or is there room? Propose 3-5 long-tail phrasings (audience +
  occasion + style/software) a new listing could target."
Expected evidence: "Head-vs-longtail verdict, the title/tag phrasings top listings use, an entrenched-vs-open read, and
  3-5 concrete long-tail target phrasings."
```
- **Keep:** target locks onto a **3+ word long-tail** whose top isn't all thousand-review shops — a new listing can ride the ~14–21 day recency boost in.
- **Drop / re-word:** head terms, or tops owned by entrenched Bestseller shops. Go back and narrow.
- Build note: front-load the main keyword in the first ~40 characters of the title, and use all ~13 tags with different long-tail variants (don't repeat one set across listings).

## Shape and price

- **You can author (keep):** printable planners, trackers, worksheets, checklists, spreadsheets (Sheets/Excel/Notion), resume templates, fill-in Canva text templates.
- **You can't (drop even if demand is real):** original clipart, complex SVG cut files, hand-drawn wall art, presets — illustration/art-driven, beyond a design-light author.
- **Price anchors:** basic A4 tracker ~$3–6; planner/journal ~$5–10; spreadsheets up to ~$35; Canva templates ~$10–40; a 3-pack bundle lifts order value. Don't price at $1.

## Etsy quirks to remember

- Favorites are a weak signal — collecting isn't paying. Judge on "X sold" and estimated revenue, not favorites.
- Third-party sales estimates (EverBee etc.) are directional (~80% accurate), not exact.
- E1→E5 usually run on the *same candidate keyword* as one pipeline (searches → paid + gap → rankability → anti-generic → shape), not as five separate switches.
