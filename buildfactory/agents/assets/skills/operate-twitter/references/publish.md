# Publish public content

Use this playbook for original posts, threads, replies, and quote posts.

## Start from a real source

Build from evidence the company actually has: a product change, customer
question, field observation, useful artifact, result, mistake, decision, or
specific opinion earned through the work. Do not manufacture activity from a
generic content calendar when there is no useful thing to say.

Read the account's recent live posts before drafting. Check for duplicate
claims, unfinished threads, an already-promised follow-up, and a voice or
positioning change that company memory has not yet caught up with.

## Pick the shape that serves the result

- Use one post when one complete idea is enough.
- Use a thread when the sequence itself carries necessary evidence or steps;
  each post must still make sense in the conversation.
- Use a reply when the value depends on the existing conversation.
- Use a quote post when the added commentary can stand on its own and the
  quoted post is necessary context.

Do not choose a longer format merely to look substantial. Do not force a link,
image, hashtag, or call to action into every post.

## Compose with existing skills

Use company voice and customer language already in `/company`. Invoke
`mine-customer-voice` when fresh audience language is actually missing. Run
`de-ai-ify` on the final public copy. For visual content, use `design-asset` or
`gen-image`, then `visual-iterate` before upload.

Those skills own writing and visual quality. This playbook owns the decision to
publish, live account context, and correct execution.

## Publish and verify

Immediately before submission, confirm the current account and re-read the
final text/media in the composer. For a thread, publish in the intended order
and keep the canonical URL of the root.

After publishing, open the public profile or exact conversation URL. Confirm:

- the content exists under the intended account;
- text, order, media, link, reply/quote target, and visibility match the intent;
- no duplicate submission occurred.

Persist only durable facts: what was published when it matters later, the live
URL when it is a useful reference, real feedback, a promise made, and the next
learning question. Do not call expected reach a result.
