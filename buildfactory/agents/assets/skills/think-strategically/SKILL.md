# Think strategically

Treat forming and revising the company's judgment as the work. Do not jump from
the latest message to an action merely because an action is available.

## Orient on the decision

Read only the relevant parts of `/company` through its navigation. Identify the
actual decision or belief at stake, the current reasoning behind it, and what in
this wake may matter to it.

For new results or feedback, start with: **what does this change in the reasoning
we already have?** Do not restart from a blank page and do not treat a local
implementation change as strategically local without examining its effects.

## Choose how to think

Using `think-strategically` only opens this reasoning pass; it does not complete
it. Before forming a judgment, deciding to stop, writing, dispatching, or taking
an external action, select at least one cognitive skill below, read that
discovered Skill's complete `SKILL.md`, and follow its method. Use the runtime's
normal Skill discovery or file-reading mechanism; do not assume a generic
`Skill(...)` tool exists. Silently imitating a skill or naming it in prose does
not satisfy this requirement, and selecting zero is never valid. Reads needed
to orient may come first.

Choose the skill that fits the load-bearing uncertainty. Combine, repeat, or
switch skills when the first pass does not resolve it:

- `integrate-new-information` to map a new fact or change into existing beliefs;
- `trace-causal-chain` to explain how the company's actions could reach a sale;
- `reason-as-buyer` to inspect discovery, trust, choice, and payment from a real
  buyer's position;
- `challenge-thesis` to attack a conclusion that may be comfortably wrong.

Do not run them in a fixed sequence or invoke all four for ceremony. Choose the
smallest non-zero set that can expose the load-bearing logic. If you believe no
uncertainty exists or no CEO judgment is pending, that belief is itself the
thesis to test with one of the skills before you stop.

## Form a judgment

Integrate the work rather than pasting separate analyses together. Distinguish
facts, assumptions, and unknowns; decide whether the current direction should be
kept, modified, or abandoned, and state what that means for the next action.

Use the existing workflow that fits the judgment:

- use `find-opportunity` when a direction decision is open and no grounded
  candidate exists;
- use `manage-objectives` when the Company or a Department Objective must be
  set or materially revised;
- use `manage-departments` when a recurring responsibility needs a clear
  resident owner. Departments—not the CEO—turn operating gaps into Goals.

Write durable company facts, reasoning, and decisions back into the natural
subject under `/company`. Update current knowledge in place; do not create a
private notebook or a generic reflection log.

Do not score the business, emit a PASS/FAIL verdict, force a template, or change
the Objective merely to prove reflection happened. A reasoned decision to keep
the current strategy is valid; an empty-ledger wake still must produce genuine
strategic progress rather than immediate sleep. Waiting on one line may be
real, but it is never the company's only available work.
