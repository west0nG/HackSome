# Integrate new information

Compare the new information with the current facts, assumptions, causal logic,
and decision already recorded for the company. Identify the exact delta before
forming a new plan.

Trace its impact:

- what existing fact, assumption, or unknown it confirms, weakens, replaces, or
  leaves untouched;
- which downstream conclusions depend on that changed element;
- whether the effect is local, changes the offer or business form, or invalidates
  the standing direction;
- what remains true and should not be needlessly rebuilt.

Small changes still deserve an impact judgment; they need not change the product
decision. Large-looking results may also leave the strategy intact when they do
not touch a load-bearing premise. Do not equate “new” with “start over,” and do
not preserve an old conclusion merely because the company has invested in it.

Return a reasoned keep, modify, or reconsider implication to the calling
strategic process. If causal or buyer impact remains unclear, identify that need
so the caller can select another cognitive skill.

When the delta creates durable company knowledge, update the relevant current
subject under `/company` rather than appending a separate reflection log. Do not
issue a verdict or run the Objective workflow yourself.
