# Challenge the thesis

State the current conclusion in its strongest fair form, then temporarily assume
it may be wrong. Look for the most consequential reason it fails, not a generic
list of risks.

Probe for:

- facts that contradict the conclusion or came from the wrong user, channel, or
  context;
- alternative explanations that fit the same evidence;
- assumptions disguised as facts by a coherent story;
- evidence borrowed from a competitor, adjacent market, or former business form;
- dependencies the company cannot satisfy;
- changes that removed a premise while leaving the old conclusion in place;
- failure paths that make local execution success commercially irrelevant.

Ask what must be true for the thesis to work, what would be observable if it
were false, and whether the current evidence can distinguish those worlds.
Separate a repairable execution weakness from a broken strategic premise.

Do not oppose the thesis for style. If it survives the strongest attack, say why
and identify the uncertainty that remains. Do not issue PASS/FAIL or modify the
Objective yourself; return a sharper judgment for the CEO to integrate.
