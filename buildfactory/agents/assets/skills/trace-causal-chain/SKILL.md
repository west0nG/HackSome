# Trace a causal chain

Take one consequential claim and explain how it could become true. Anchor the
chain in known facts and actions the company can actually execute, then follow
the effects to the commercial outcome that matters.

Interrogate every important link:

- what causes the next step rather than merely appearing beside it;
- which evidence supports that mechanism;
- which link is an assumption or unknown;
- which company capability, dependency, channel, or buyer behavior it requires;
- what would happen downstream if the link failed or changed.

Reason both forward from the company's action and backward from the sale. Include
product efficacy when it affects belief, trust, reviews, repeat purchase, or
another load-bearing commercial link; do not turn it into a separate universal
gate.

Business forms have different chains. Do not force every service, content play,
information product, software product, or commerce offer through one funnel.
Pay special attention to couplings: changing a channel, delivery method, or buyer
may also change whether the offer itself makes sense.

Surface the weakest or unsupported transitions and what evidence could change
the conclusion. Do not collapse the result into a score or verdict; return the
causal logic so the CEO can judge what to keep, reshape, or abandon.
